Glossary

Communicative Competence - The user's ability to understand and engage in a language by learning grammatical patterns and actively practicing.


Individualism-Collectivism - The way that individuals and gropus relate to and influence each other. Whether a culture values freedom, personal space, individuality, versus the collectivist value of experiencing the world through social and familial groups.


Universal-Particular - Applies to the developed rules and norms of a culture. Universal cultures maintain definitions of good and right upon which society acts. Particular cultures place more emphasis on the context of morality and situational details.


High Context-Low Context - How a culture values communication. High context cultures communicate through language that relies on prior knowledge, while low context communication focuses less on the additional context.


Multiliteracy - Defines the skills needed to navigate hypertext in social, cultural, and methodological aspects.
